✨ Gemini

Google's AI assistant — available free to all Diocese staff through the Google Workspace for Nonprofits licence. The everyday AI for drafting, summarising, creating images, and getting things done.

What is Gemini?

Gemini is Google's AI assistant — similar in concept to asking a very knowledgeable colleague for help. You type a question or request in plain English and it responds with a draft, summary, answer, or whatever you've asked for.

It's available free to all Diocese staff as part of the Google Workspace for Nonprofits licence. You access it at gemini.google.com — but always through your Diocese Google account, not a personal Gmail.

Gemini draws on a broad base of general knowledge and can help with a wide range of everyday writing and thinking tasks. It's the right tool for most general tasks. For deep analysis of specific documents, see NotebookLM. For complex task automation and M365 integration, managers should use Claude + Cowork.

What it can do
✉️
Drafting emails and letters
Give Gemini the key points and tone you need, and it will produce a well-written first draft in seconds. Works for formal Diocese correspondence, replies to enquiries, or internal communications.
✍️
Grammar and proofreading
Paste in a draft and ask Gemini to check it for grammar, spelling, clarity, or tone. It will suggest improvements or produce a corrected version.
📋
Summarising documents
Paste in a long document, report, or email chain and ask for a summary. Gemini can produce a short paragraph or a bullet-point overview — whichever is most useful.
💡
Brainstorming and ideas
Stuck on how to structure a report, phrase a tricky message, or approach a problem? Ask Gemini for ideas or options. It's good at generating starting points for you to refine.
📝
Creating agendas and minutes templates
Describe a meeting or committee and ask Gemini to draft an agenda structure or a template for minutes. Saves time getting started on routine documents.
🔍
Research and plain-English explanations
Ask Gemini to explain a concept, policy, or regulation in plain English — useful for understanding legislation, Church of England guidance, or unfamiliar topics quickly.
🌍
Translation
Need to communicate with a parishioner or contact in another language? Gemini can translate text to and from a wide range of languages quickly and accurately.
📊
Formatting and restructuring text
Ask Gemini to turn bullet points into flowing paragraphs, reformat a table into a summary, or restructure a document into a different layout or style.
🖼️
Image creation
Gemini can generate images from a text description using Google's Imagen technology. Useful for creating visuals for newsletters, event flyers, social media, or presentations — especially alongside tools like Canva where you can edit the result further.
🎬
Video generation
Gemini (via Google's Veo model) can create short video clips from a text description. Useful for social media content, communication campaigns, or simple explainer visuals. Clips are typically a few seconds long and can be downloaded and used directly.

What it can't do — and where to be careful

Gemini's limitations

  • ⚠️
    It can make things up — confidently This is called "hallucination." Gemini draws on broad training data and can occasionally produce plausible-sounding information that is incorrect, out of date, or simply invented. Always verify facts, figures, dates, and names before including them in official communications. Never rely on Gemini for legal, financial, or medical specifics without checking.
  • 📅
    Its knowledge has a cutoff date Gemini's training data has a cutoff — it may not know about very recent events, policy changes, or news. For current affairs or recent Church of England guidance, check primary sources directly.
  • 📂
    It doesn't automatically access your Diocese files Gemini (via the web) doesn't have access to your Outlook emails, SharePoint, or OneDrive. You need to paste in the content you want it to work on. If you need Claude to search your inbox or open files directly from OneDrive, that's what Claude + Cowork is for — it connects directly to your Diocese Microsoft account. For deep analysis of specific uploaded documents, NotebookLM is also worth considering.
  • 🔒
    It doesn't know Diocese-specific context Gemini has no knowledge of internal Diocese processes, people, history, or preferences unless you tell it. Treat each conversation as starting fresh — provide the context it needs.
  • 🧮
    It can struggle with complex numbers and calculations For straightforward sums it's fine, but don't rely on Gemini for detailed financial calculations or complex data analysis. Always check the arithmetic independently.
💡
The golden rule: Gemini is an excellent first-draft tool. Always read, check, and take responsibility for anything you send or publish. Think of it as a capable assistant who occasionally gets things wrong — you're always the editor.
Getting started

How to use Gemini — step by step


1
Check your account first
Go to gemini.google.com. Look at the account icon in the top-right corner. It must show your @carlislediocese.org.uk address. If it shows a personal Gmail, click the icon, sign out, and sign back in with your Diocese account.
2
Type your request in plain English
Don't worry about using special commands or keywords — just describe what you need as you'd ask a colleague. The more context you give, the better the result. "Draft a short reply to thank a volunteer" is fine; "draft a 150-word email thanking a volunteer for running the Christmas fair, warm but professional in tone" is better.
3
Refine if needed
If the first response isn't quite right, don't start over — just reply and tell Gemini what to change. "Make it shorter", "make the tone more formal", "add a line about the event date" — all work naturally.
4
Always review before using
Read the response carefully. Check any facts, names, or figures it has included. Edit as needed. You are always responsible for the final content — Gemini is the assistant, not the author.
Privacy & data

🔒 Your data is protected — when you use the right account

When accessed via your Diocese Google Workspace account, Gemini is covered by Google's Cloud Data Processing Addendum. Your conversations are not used to train Google's AI models, and Google acts as a data processor on behalf of the Diocese. The Diocese owns your data.

When accessed via a personal Gmail account, Google's consumer terms apply. By default, your conversations may be used to improve Google's AI. This is why the account check is so important.

The Diocese is the data controller under UK GDPR — which means staff are responsible for what personal data they put into Gemini, regardless of how strong Google's protections are. Apply the same judgement you'd use with any trusted external professional.

🚦 What to put in — and what not to
🟢 Generally fine
Drafting and proofreading letters, emails, and communications · Summarising documents or reports · Researching topics or explaining concepts · Creating templates, agendas, and standard documents · Formatting and restructuring text
🟡 Use with care — think before you paste
Named individuals' personal details — anonymise where possible (use "a parishioner" rather than their name) · Personnel matters — templates are fine; specific named employee situations need more thought
🔴 Do not put into Gemini
Safeguarding case notes or referral details about identifiable individuals · Detailed medical, health, or pastoral confidences about named people · Passwords, login credentials, or financial account information
