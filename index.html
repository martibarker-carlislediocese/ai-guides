AI Guides

Practical guides to using AI tools across the Diocese — written for each team, in plain English, with data privacy at the centre.

⚠️ Only use Diocese-approved tools for work tasks

Do not use ChatGPT, Perplexity, or other free AI tools for any Diocese work — including drafting, summarising, or sharing any information about staff, parishes, or the people we serve.

Do not use a personal paid subscription (such as your own ChatGPT Plus or Claude account) for Diocese tasks. Even a paid personal account does not carry the same contractual protections the Diocese has negotiated. There is no Data Processing Agreement in place, your data may be used to train the AI provider's models, and the Diocese cannot fulfil its UK GDPR obligations if staff are using unapproved tools.

✗ ChatGPT ✗ Perplexity ✗ Personal AI subscriptions ✗ Other unapproved AI tools

The tools approved for Diocese use — listed below — all have formal Data Processing Agreements in place and do not use your data for AI training. These protections only apply when you use the Diocese accounts and licences.

Also Available — With Limitations
🪟
Microsoft Copilot
Free version included with M365 Business Premium  ·  All staff
⚠ Use with caution — lower accuracy

Microsoft Copilot Chat is bundled with the Diocese's Microsoft 365 Business Premium licence, so staff may already be using it. It is permitted, but it is the least capable of the Diocese's available AI tools and should not be the first choice for important work.

What the free version can do: General chat and question-answering, drafting basic text, web-grounded searches. Similar to a basic chatbot.

What it cannot do (without a paid £30/user/month add-on): access your Diocese emails, SharePoint files, Teams messages, or OneDrive documents. The free version has no connection to your Diocese data.

Recommendation: For any Diocese work, use Gemini (free, better accuracy, Diocese DPA in place) or Claude + Cowork (Diocese Team licence, most powerful). Use Copilot only if you have a specific reason to.

📊 How do they compare for accuracy?

~40% Copilot citation error rate — the highest of the three tools (Columbia Journalism Review, March 2025)
✔ Gemini — significantly better
Performs well on drafting and summarisation tasks. Strong choice for everyday Diocese work (Vectara, 2025).
✔✔ Claude Teams — fewest hallucinations
Produces the fewest factual errors of the three tools across 2025 benchmarks — the most reliable for research and knowledge-intensive work (Vectara, 2025; AA-Omniscience, 2025).

Sources: Columbia Journalism Review citation accuracy study (March 2025); Vectara Hallucination Leaderboard (Nov 2025); AA-Omniscience Knowledge Benchmark (2025). Rates vary significantly by task type — always review AI output before use.
