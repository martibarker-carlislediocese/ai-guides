📓 NotebookLM

Google's document intelligence tool — not a general AI assistant, but a focused tool for understanding and querying the documents that matter to you.

What is NotebookLM?

NotebookLM is a free tool from Google that lets you upload your own documents — reports, PDFs, meeting notes, policy papers — and then ask questions about them in plain English. It reads your documents and answers based only on what you've given it.

Think of it as a very well-read assistant who has studied exactly the documents on your desk and nothing else. It won't add information from the internet or make things up — if the answer isn't in your documents, it will tell you so.

It's available free to all Diocese staff through the Google Workspace for Nonprofits account at notebooklm.google.com.

🎙️ The Audio Overview — a standout feature

One of NotebookLM's most distinctive features is Audio Overview. Once you've uploaded your sources, NotebookLM can generate a short podcast-style conversation between two AI hosts summarising the key points of your documents. It's particularly useful for getting a quick feel for a long report during a commute, or sharing a summary with colleagues who haven't read the papers. The audio is generated from your documents only — nothing external is added.

Getting started

How to use NotebookLM — step by step

1
Go to NotebookLM
Open notebooklm.google.com — make sure you're signed in with your Diocese Google Workspace account (your @carlislediocese.org.uk address), not a personal Gmail account.
2
Create a new notebook
Click New Notebook. Give it a name that makes sense — for example "PCC Meeting — March 2026" or "Safeguarding Policy 2025".
3
Upload your sources
Click Add Sources. You can upload PDFs, Word documents, Google Docs, paste in text, or add a URL. You can add up to 50 sources per notebook. NotebookLM will read and index them — this takes about 30 seconds.
4
Ask your questions
Type your question in the chat box — exactly as you'd ask a colleague. For example: "What are the main agenda items for this meeting?" or "What does the policy say about lone working?" NotebookLM will answer and show you which source it drew from.
5
Generate an Audio Overview (optional)
Click Audio Overview in the right panel and then Generate. Within a couple of minutes, NotebookLM produces a short podcast summarising your sources. You can listen in the browser or download it.

🔒 Privacy & data — what you need to know

Your sources are not used to train AI models. Google's terms for NotebookLM (accessed via a Google Workspace account) explicitly state that your uploaded documents and queries are not used to improve Google's AI. A Data Processing Addendum covers the Diocese's use.

Your notebook is private. Only you can see the sources you upload and the conversations you have, unless you choose to share a notebook with a colleague.

Always use your Diocese Workspace account. Sign in at notebooklm.google.com with your @carlislediocese.org.uk address. If you use a personal Gmail account, the consumer privacy terms apply and your data may be used for training.

Apply the same judgement as any external service. NotebookLM is safe for most document work — board papers, reports, policy documents. As with any AI tool, avoid uploading documents containing individual safeguarding case details, detailed personal health information, or anything you would not share with a trusted professional adviser. For queries, contact Marti Barker.
