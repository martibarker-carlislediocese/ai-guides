Diocese of Carlisle — AI Guides

⚖️ Responsible Use of AI

A plain-English guide to using AI tools wisely — applying across Gemini, NotebookLM, Microsoft Copilot, and Claude + Cowork.

AI tools offer genuine benefits for Diocese staff — saving time on drafting, summarising, and routine tasks. But using them responsibly is important, both to protect the people we serve and to comply with our obligations under UK data protection law.

This page sets out the Diocese's approach. The rules here apply to all approved AI tools — Gemini, NotebookLM, Microsoft Copilot, and Claude + Cowork. Each tool also has its own guide with more detail.

If you're ever unsure, the question to ask yourself is: "Would I be comfortable if the Bishop or our data protection officer could see exactly what I've just shared with this tool?" If yes, proceed. If not, don't.

Core principles
01
You are always responsible
AI tools are assistants, not decision-makers. Everything you send, publish, or act upon remains your responsibility. Always read, check, and edit AI output before using it. Never send something you haven't reviewed.
02
Use the right account
Always use your Diocese account when accessing AI tools — never a personal email or Google account. The data protections negotiated by the Diocese only apply when you're signed in as yourself within the Diocese's licences.
03
Don't share what you wouldn't share with HR, IT, or the Diocesan Secretary
A useful test: would you be comfortable sharing this information with HR, IT, or the Diocesan Secretary? If yes, it's likely fine for AI tools. If not, keep it out of the tool entirely.
04
AI can be wrong — check the facts
All AI tools can produce plausible-sounding information that is incorrect, out of date, or invented. This is called "hallucination." Always verify names, facts, figures, and legal or policy details from primary sources before including them in official documents.
05
Protect vulnerable people above all
Safeguarding information, case notes, and personal details about vulnerable individuals must never be shared with any AI tool. The Diocese's safeguarding obligations are absolute and take precedence over any productivity benefit.
06
Use the right tool for the task
Different tools are approved for different uses. Gemini for everyday drafting. NotebookLM for document analysis. Claude + Cowork for managers needing automation. Microsoft Copilot (basic) is available but has significant limitations — see the tool guides.
What to share — and what not to

This applies to all approved AI tools. When in doubt, err on the side of sharing less.

🟢 Generally fine — use AI tools for these
Drafting letters, emails and communications Grammar checking and proofreading Summarising reports and documents Creating templates and standard documents Agenda and minute templates Researching topics and explaining concepts Financial reports and budget summaries Translating text Formatting and restructuring documents Brainstorming ideas and approaches
🟡 Use with care — think before you paste
Named individuals' personal details — consider whether the person's name is necessary, or whether "a parishioner" or "a member of staff" achieves the same result. Use the minimum personal information needed to get the task done.

Personnel and HR matters — letter templates are fine. Drafting content about a specific named individual's conduct, absence, or personal circumstances warrants more care. Anonymise where possible.

Sensitive financial details — general budget summaries are fine. Specific account numbers, payroll details, or individual salary information should not be shared.
🔴 Never share with any AI tool
Safeguarding case notes or referrals Medical or health information about individuals Pastoral confidences shared in confidence Passwords, PINs or login credentials Financial account or banking details Full national insurance or ID numbers

UK GDPR & data protection

⚖️ What the law requires

The Diocese is the data controller under UK GDPR for any personal data that staff put into AI tools. This means the Diocese — and individual staff members — are responsible for ensuring that personal data is processed lawfully, fairly, and securely.

All three primary tools have Data Processing Agreements in place. Google (Gemini and NotebookLM), Anthropic (Claude), and Microsoft (Copilot) all contractually commit to acting as data processors on behalf of the Diocese and not using your data to train their AI models — when accessed via the correct accounts.

You must have a lawful basis to process personal data. For most Diocese admin tasks, "legitimate interests" covers general correspondence and document work. But processing special category data (health, religious beliefs, safeguarding) requires a specific basis and extra care — in most cases, this means keeping it out of AI tools entirely.

When in doubt, contact the Diocese's Data Protection Officer — or Marti Barker (IT Support Manager) for day-to-day queries about AI tool use.

📋
Reporting concerns: If you think you may have shared something inappropriately with an AI tool, contact Marti Barker as soon as possible. It's always better to raise a concern early than to leave it unreported.
