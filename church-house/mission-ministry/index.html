Church House — AI Guide

🕊️ Mission & Ministry

Supporting parish ministry, events, retreat programmes, and diocesan resources — with AI handling the administrative and creative work so your team can focus on the ministry itself.

The Mission and Ministry team produces a constant flow of resources, communications, and event management work — retreat programmes, training events, parish guidance, liturgical materials, and outreach resources. Much of this is creative and contextual work where AI is genuinely useful as a drafting and organising partner, without replacing the theological judgement and pastoral sensitivity that underpins it.

Gemini is strong for creative drafting, summarising theological documents, and producing event materials. Claude + Cowork adds the ability to manage event bookings through Outlook, organise resources on SharePoint, and handle the administrative cycle around retreats and training days.

Gemini — resources, events and communications

✨ Gemini

Open in your browser · No setup · All staff

Draft service orders and liturgy outlines

Give Gemini the theme, season, and readings and it will produce a structured service order — with suggested prayers, responses, and a sermon outline. A strong first draft that your team shapes around the specific occasion and community.

Try this prompt "Draft a service order for an Advent Sunday morning service suitable for a mixed congregation including families. Theme: waiting and hope. Readings: Isaiah 40:1-11, Mark 1:1-8. Include a children's address outline."

Create retreat and training day programmes

Describe the theme, audience, and length of your retreat, and Gemini will produce a structured programme — session titles, timings, suggested activities, and reflective questions. A useful scaffold for any planning meeting.

Write event publicity and promotional materials

Gemini can draft posters, social media posts, email announcements, and parish bulletin notices for any event — matching the tone and audience you specify. Multiple versions for different channels from one brief.

Try this prompt "Write a parish bulletin announcement and a short social media post for a Quiet Day on [date] at [venue], themed around Lenten reflection. Audience: regular churchgoers aged 40+. Keep the bulletin version to 80 words."

Summarise theological documents and reports

When the Church of England or Diocese publishes new reports — on evangelism, discipleship, or ministry strategy — paste the key sections into Gemini and ask for a plain-English summary suitable for sharing with parish clergy or lay leaders.

Write grant applications for ministry projects

Describe the project and Gemini will help structure a grant application — articulating the need, the proposed activity, the expected outcomes, and how you will evaluate success. A much faster starting point than a blank page.

🖥️ Claude + Cowork

Opens your files · Works with Outlook and SharePoint · Things Gemini can't do

Manage event bookings via Outlook

When people email to book a retreat, Quiet Day, or training event, Claude can read your Outlook inbox, compile the booking list, check for capacity, send confirmation emails with joining instructions, and flag any special requirements mentioned — all without you maintaining a manual spreadsheet.

Example workflow "Read my inbox for emails about the October Quiet Day at Rydal Hall. Create a booking register with names and contact details. Send confirmation emails to everyone who has booked, using the standard joining instructions template."
⏱ Managing 25 retreat bookings: 3 hours manually → 20 minutes with Claude.

Organise ministry resources on SharePoint

Claude can help bring order to a SharePoint resource library — renaming files consistently, moving resources into logical folders by theme or season, and generating a structured index so clergy and parish workers can find what they need without asking.

Chase non-responders and send reminders

Claude can compare your booking list against your Outlook contacts to identify who has been invited but not responded, then draft and send reminder emails — personalised with each person's name — ready for your review before sending.

Generate ministry statistics reports

If annual ministry returns are stored in SharePoint or submitted by email, Claude can read them, aggregate the key figures, and produce a summary report — showing attendance trends, baptisms, marriages, and ministry activity across the Diocese — without you collating each return manually.

⏱ Annual statistics compilation: days → hours.

Create consistent parish communication templates

Claude can take a standard letter or email template from SharePoint and produce personalised versions for every parish in the Diocese — inserting the correct incumbent name, parish name, and deanery — ready to send via Outlook as a batch.

A note on AI and theological content: AI tools can produce useful first drafts of liturgy, prayers, and theological commentary — but they draw on existing published material and may not reflect the Diocese's particular theological tradition or the pastoral context of a specific community. Always review AI-generated liturgical content carefully, and treat it as a starting point for your own creative and pastoral work, not as a finished product.
