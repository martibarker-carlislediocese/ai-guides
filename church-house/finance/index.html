Church House — AI Guide

💰 Finance

How AI tools can reduce time spent on routine financial reporting, correspondence, and analysis — so your team can focus on the work that requires human judgement.

Much of what the Finance team produces — management account narratives, grant reports, trustee updates, supplier correspondence — follows a consistent structure every month. AI won't replace the judgement involved in financial decision-making, but it can draft the words, spot anomalies in spreadsheet data, and handle the routine correspondence that consumes hours every cycle.

There are two tools available to you. Gemini is the everyday AI — open it in your browser and start typing. Claude + Cowork goes further: it can open files directly from your computer, read your Sage 200 Excel exports, and work with Outlook — without you copying and pasting anything.

Start here — Gemini for everyday finance tasks

✨ Gemini

Open in your browser · No setup · All staff

Write management account commentary

Paste in a table of figures and ask Gemini to write the explanatory narrative. It will describe variances, flag movements, and frame the commentary in professional language — ready for you to review and adjust.

Try this prompt "Here is our income and expenditure for March versus budget. Write a 3-paragraph management commentary explaining the main variances, in plain English suitable for our Finance Committee."

Draft trustee and PCC financial reports

Gemini can turn a set of figures into a readable narrative report. Describe what happened — which income lines moved and why — and it will write the report around your explanation.

Explain accounting concepts in plain English

Need to explain restricted funds, the Charities Act requirements, or why the balance sheet has changed to a trustee who isn't an accountant? Gemini can translate financial language into clear prose.

Try this prompt "Explain the difference between restricted and unrestricted funds to a church warden who has no accounting background."

Write grant application financial sections

Paste in your budget figures and let Gemini draft the supporting narrative for grant applications — explaining what the money is for, how it will be spent, and how you will account for it.

Draft financial policy templates

Ask Gemini to draft a expenses policy, a purchasing policy, or a reserves policy — then adapt it to the Diocese's specifics. A first draft in minutes rather than starting from a blank page.

🖥️ Claude + Cowork

Opens your files · Works with Outlook · Things Gemini can't do

Read your Sage 200 Excel exports

Sage 200 doesn't connect directly to AI — but your Excel exports do. Export your month-end trial balance, aged creditors, or any Sage report to Excel, and Claude can open it directly, read across every row and column, and do the analysis for you.

Example workflow Export your trial balance from Sage 200. Tell Claude: "Open this file and flag any expense line that has moved by more than 15% versus the same period last year, then draft a one-sentence explanation I can review for each." Done in under a minute.
⏱ What takes 45 minutes of manual comparison takes 2 minutes with Claude.

Work across your linked Excel workbooks

If your budget model spans multiple linked Excel sheets — common when workbooks pull figures from Sage — Claude can open the master file, navigate the linked sheets, read the totals across all tabs, and produce a summary. It will also flag if a link appears broken or a cell is showing an error value.

Generate month-end narrative from your actual figures

Point Claude at your month-end Excel file and ask it to draft the management account commentary directly from the numbers — no copy-pasting figures into a chat window. Claude reads the file and writes the narrative using the actual data.

Example workflow "Open March_Management_Accounts.xlsx and write a Finance Committee narrative explaining income versus budget, expenditure movements, and the overall surplus/deficit position."

Process supplier and grant correspondence via Outlook

Claude can search your Outlook inbox for emails relating to a specific supplier or grant, pull them together into a timeline of what's been agreed and what's outstanding, and draft a chasing email or acknowledgement — without you scrolling through months of emails.

⏱ A 30-minute inbox trawl becomes a 2-minute task.

Maintain a grant tracker on SharePoint

Claude can read a SharePoint grant tracking spreadsheet and, when you receive a new grant email in Outlook, help you update the tracker, note key deadlines, and draft a receipt acknowledgement — all from one instruction.

💡 A note on Sage 200

Claude cannot connect directly to Sage 200's database — it works with the files you export from it. The good news is that almost everything Finance needs — trial balances, aged reports, budget comparisons, payroll summaries — can be exported from Sage to Excel in seconds. From that point, Claude can do the analytical and narrative work that would otherwise take you significant time.

If your team works with Excel spreadsheets that pull data directly from Sage via linked cells, Claude can open these too. It reads the values as they appear in the spreadsheet and can work with whatever data is visible, including spotting where links have broken or where figures look unusual.

🔒 Data safety for Finance

Fine to share with AI tools

  • Aggregated income and expenditure figures
  • Budget comparisons and variance analysis
  • Grant narratives and application text
  • Policy templates and draft documents
  • Report commentary and trustee updates
  • Supplier correspondence (general)

Do not share with any AI tool

  • Individual staff salary or payroll details
  • Bank account or sort code numbers
  • Full supplier account or banking details
  • National insurance or tax reference numbers
  • Individual expense claims with personal details
  • Passwords or login credentials for any system
