Church House — AI Guide

🤝 Safeguarding

Strict guidelines on what AI can and cannot be used for — with practical support for training administration, policy work, and SharePoint organisation.

⛔ This must be read first — absolute limits for Safeguarding

Safeguarding work involves some of the most sensitive personal data the Diocese holds. The rules below are absolute — they apply to every AI tool, including Claude + Cowork, Gemini, and any other system. There are no exceptions, regardless of how urgent the task feels.

Case notes or referral details of any kind
Names or identifiers of individuals subject to safeguarding concerns
Details of abuse allegations or investigations
Information that could identify a victim or alleged perpetrator
DBS check results or criminal record information
Multi-Agency Safeguarding Hub (MASH) or referral information
Pastoral or private disclosures shared in confidence
Children's personal details of any kind

If you are ever unsure whether something is safe to share with an AI tool, the answer is: don't. Contact Marti Barker (IT Support Manager) if you need guidance.

Within those firm limits, there is genuine value AI can offer the Safeguarding team — particularly around the administrative work that surrounds safeguarding rather than the casework itself. Training course management, policy document drafting, guidance materials for parishes, and the SharePoint organisation that underpins it all are all appropriate uses.

The Safeguarding team runs a significant volume of training and certification activity across the Diocese. Managing bookings, confirmations, registers, and certificate tracking is time-consuming. This is exactly where AI earns its keep — handling the admin so your team can focus on the work that matters.

What AI can legitimately help with

✨ Gemini

Open in your browser · No case data ever

Draft training course materials and guidance

Gemini can help write or improve safeguarding training descriptions, joining instructions, delegate briefings, and parish guidance documents. All of this is generic — no case information, no named individuals.

Try this prompt "Write a parish safeguarding officer briefing document explaining the Diocese's expectations for safeguarding in a local church setting. Use plain language suitable for volunteers."

Summarise Church of England guidance updates

When new safeguarding guidance is published by the Church of England or statutory bodies, paste it into Gemini and ask for a plain-English summary — so you can quickly understand what's changed and communicate it to parishes.

Draft FAQ documents for Parish Safeguarding Officers

Ask Gemini to draft a Q&A document for PSOs covering common situations — when to report, who to contact, what the Diocese expects. Gemini produces a clear first draft that you refine based on your expertise.

Write policy templates and procedural flowcharts

Gemini can help draft safeguarding policy documents, review and improve existing policies for clarity, and describe procedures in a step-by-step format — always working with generic policy language, never case-specific content.

Structure safeguarding audit and self-assessment frameworks

Ask Gemini to create a structured self-assessment template for parishes, based on the Church of England's safeguarding framework — with clear questions, response options, and guidance notes.

🖥️ Claude + Cowork

Training admin · SharePoint · Outlook · No case data ever

Manage training course bookings via Outlook

When people email to book a training course, Claude can read your Outlook inbox, compile the booking list, send confirmation emails with joining instructions, and flag when you're approaching capacity — without you maintaining a spreadsheet manually.

Example workflow "Read my Outlook inbox for emails about the May Domestic Abuse Awareness training. Create a booking register with names and organisations, and draft confirmation emails with the joining instructions attached."
⏱ Managing 30 bookings manually: 2 hours. With Claude: 15 minutes.

Create and send joining instructions automatically

Claude can generate personalised joining instructions for each delegate from a standard template — inserting their name, the course date and location, what to bring, and any preparatory reading — then send them via Outlook as a batch.

Organise your SharePoint training records

Claude can help bring order to a SharePoint folder structure — renaming files consistently, moving documents into the right folders, and generating a summary of what training records exist and where gaps might be. This is especially useful if SharePoint feels unfamiliar or overwhelming.

⏱ SharePoint reorganisation that would take a day can be done in under an hour.

Track and flag expiring certifications

Point Claude at a training records spreadsheet on SharePoint and ask it to identify everyone whose certification is due to expire in the next 3 months — and draft individual reminder emails to each person, ready for you to review and send.

Build a training attendance register

After a course, Claude can read the booking list and generate a formatted attendance register — with a column for signature, a column to mark who attended, and a summary of non-attendees for follow-up.

📅 Training management — where AI saves the most time

The Safeguarding team coordinates training across the whole Diocese — courses, certifications, renewals, and the administrative cycle that goes with each. This is high-volume, repetitive work where AI is genuinely transformative.

A typical training cycle — announce course, receive bookings, confirm delegates, send joining instructions, create register, chase non-attenders, update records — can be largely automated using Claude + Cowork and Outlook. You set the parameters, Claude handles the correspondence and record-keeping, and you review before anything is sent.

None of this involves case information. It's purely administrative — which is exactly why it's safe to use AI for.
